• Monument of Jesus Christ in Brazil •

Christ the Redeemer is a monument of Jesus Christ, religious leader and greatest representative of Christianity. The statue with open arms represents peace and love and It is one of the most visited places in Brazil and the world.

The beauty, religious and cultural representation of the monument inspired many Brazilian artists to compose their songs. The singer Vitor Teixeira, better known as Teixeirinha, composed the song "Percorrendo o Meu País", and referenced the statue. Look:

Christ the Redeemer was inaugurated on October 12, 1931. This date is one of the most important for Catholicism in the country, when the day of the patroness of Brazil - Nossa Senhora Aparecida - is celebrated.

The location of the monument is in one of the most exuberant places in the city of Rio de Janeiro, known as the wonderful city. Christ the Redeemer is located on Corcovado Mountain, in the neighborhood of Santa Tereza and close to the Tijuca National Park.

In Art Deco style, Christ the Redeemer is 38 meters high (including the pedestal) and 28 meters wide. Christ is located 709 meters above sea level and its weight is 1145 tons. It is the second largest monument of Christ in the world, second only to the Statue of Christ the King in Poland.
